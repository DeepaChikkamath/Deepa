Now that I am

It was sunny and bright.

I fell into the ocean, the water was clear.
I feared it.
I was scared and terrified I would die.
I was drowning.

I saw them on the boat that I fell from.
They looked sad when I looked at them.
They said, calm down. I sank down faster.
They said, breathe. They knew I was under water.
When I did not look, I felt like they were better.

So I drowned faster.
And faster.

They said, listen.
How could I? I was under water.

I sank.
Faster.
Deeper.
My chest was tight.
They said they were trying to help. I was not listening.
But I knew, they did not want to get wet under water.
I sank.
Deeper and deeper.

I reached the bed; I kicked back up.
I realised,
I am the ocean.
Everything stopped.
I can breathe.
I become one with the surface.
I embrace being the ocean.
The shine on the surface feels more illuminating.
The sun is more pink.
It is brighter than ever.
Softer than ever.
Everything is just more.

Now that I am, I did not look for the boat.
But when I did, no one was on the boat.
There was no boat.

- Deepa
